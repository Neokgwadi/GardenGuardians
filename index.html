Understanding Food Security and Community Gardens

Community gardens play a vital role in ensuring food security for vulnerable communities

What is Food Security?

Food security means that all people, at all times have access to sufficient, safe and nutritious food. It ensures that communities are not at risk of hunger, malnutrition, or food shortages.

Why Community Gardens Matter

Community gardens provide accessible, affordable, and sustainable sources of fresh produce for households, schools, and vulnerable communities.

Benefits of Community Gardens

Community gardens reduce food insecurity, promote skill development, encourage healthy eating, and support sustainable farming practices.

Sustainable Practices

Gardening raises environmental awareness and supports sustainable farming practices, contributing to a healthier ecosystem for future generations.

The Impact of Climate Change on Food Gardens

Unexpected weather events are threatening community food gardens across South Africa

Heatwaves

Dry out the soil and weaken plant growth, reducing crop yields and increasing water requirements.

Heavy Rains

Lead to flooding, root rot, and soil erosion, destroying crops and damaging garden infrastructure.

Cold Snaps & Frost

Can destroy seedlings and delay planting, shortening growing seasons and reducing harvests.

Strong Winds

Break plant stems and damage garden structures, leading to crop loss and increased maintenance costs.

Case Studies: South African Community Gardens

Real examples of how climate change is affecting food gardens across the country

Phumulani Agri-Village

Belfast, Mpumalanga

Crops Grown:

  • Organic vegetables (spinach, cabbage, carrots)

Climate Problems:

  • Severe weather damage (hailstorms, heavy rainfall)
  • Crops destroyed

Solutions Implemented:

  • Built protective greenhouses

Exxaro's Belfast mine started this project to support 195 resettled community members with farming skills and income opportunities.

eKhenana Commune

Cato Manor, Durban

Crops Grown:

  • Mixed vegetables

Climate Problems:

  • Drought and water scarcity
  • Unreliable access to municipal water

Solutions Implemented:

  • Water-saving methods
  • Rainwater and greywater collection
  • Drip irrigation systems

Founded by evicted shack dwellers to fight hunger and poverty through communal farming.

Elundini Permaculture

Elundini, Eastern Cape

Crops Grown:

  • Seasonal vegetables, spinach, herbs

Climate Problems:

  • Drought and severe water shortages
  • Limited access to running water

Solutions Implemented:

  • Water-saving methods
  • Composting and mulching
  • Permaculture design

Launched during COVID-19 to fight hunger in a remote village with severe water shortages.

Gardendale Farm

Gardendale, Western Cape

Crops Grown:

  • Vegetables and livestock

Climate Problems:

  • Extreme rainfall and floods
  • 500 chickens died, 50 pigs lost
  • Crops submerged

Solutions Implemented:

  • Improving drainage and managing water flow
  • Using raised garden beds
  • Making the garden more flood resistant

Severe floods devastated this communal farm, drowning livestock and submerging crops. The farm continues recovery efforts with improved flood resistance measures.

Orangeville Communal Vegetable Garden

Orangeville, Western Cape

Crops Grown:

  • Winter vegetables: cabbage, onions, cauliflower, potatoes

Climate Problems:

  • Severe rainfall and floods
  • Gardens submerged for months
  • Food prices soared and farming halted

Solutions Implemented:

  • Improved drainage and water flow management
  • Used compost and tilled soil to improve drainage
  • Determined to replant and recover

For two consecutive winters, severe floods destroyed vegetable gardens, washing away crops and cutting off supplies. Farmers remain committed to food security and resilience.

Practical Solutions for Garden Protection

Affordable and effective strategies to protect community gardens from climate impacts

Protective Structures

Build low-cost protective structures to shield crops from extreme weather:

  • Use shade cloths and plastic tunnels
  • Create covers to protect from heat and rain
  • Build simple greenhouses for delicate plants

Resilient Crops

Choose weather-resistant crops that can survive climate challenges:

  • Plant spinach, onions, and sweet potatoes
  • Select indigenous varieties adapted to local conditions
  • Use crop rotation to maintain soil health

Water Management

Implement water-saving techniques to combat drought:

  • Collect rainwater with household containers
  • Use drip irrigation systems
  • Practice mulching to retain soil moisture

Soil Improvement

Enhance soil quality to support plant health:

  • Make compost from kitchen waste
  • Use organic fertilizers
  • Practice crop rotation and companion planting
